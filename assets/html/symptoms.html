BRAIN STROKE SYMPTOMS

LOSS OF BALANCE

A stroke can disrupt the brain's ability to control movement, leading to sudden dizziness, unsteady walking, or difficulty maintaining posture.

EYE PROBLEMS

Sudden blurred vision, double vision, or complete vision loss in one or both eyes due to disrupted blood flow to the optic nerves.

EARLY MORNING DIZZINESS

Lightheadedness upon waking up can signal reduced blood flow to the brain, often due to a stroke affecting the cerebellum.

FACIAL WEAKNESS

One side of the face may suddenly droop, making it difficult to smile or move facial muscles, often indicating nerve damage.

EXTREME FATIGUE

Sudden and overwhelming tiredness due to the brain struggling with limited oxygen supply, leading to difficulty focusing.

ARM WEAKNESS

One arm may feel numb, weak, or difficult to lift, sometimes without the person realizing the loss of strength.

SPEECH DISTURBANCE

Speech may become slurred, slow, or difficult to understand. Some individuals struggle to find the right words (aphasia).

TERRIBLE HEADACHE

A sudden, severe headache with no known cause can indicate a hemorrhagic stroke (bleeding in the brain).

LACK OF CONCENTRATION

Difficulty focusing, processing information, or remembering things due to impaired cognitive function.
